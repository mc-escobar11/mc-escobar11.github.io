About me

Maria C. Escobar

I am a PhD candidate in Engineering and a researcher at the Center for Research and Formation in Artificial Intelligence (CINFONIA) supervised by Prof. Pablo Arbeláez at Universidad de Los Andes in Bogotá, Colombia. My main interests include Computer Vision and Deep Learning, as well as their role in human understanding through embodied AI. I am currently working on egocentric vision for 3D analysis and human pose estimation. When I’m not running experiments, I am usually crocheting, dancing or volunteering.

Bone Age Assessment

Bone Age assessment is a critical factor for determining delayed development in children, which can be a sign of pathologies such as endocrine diseases, growth abnormalities, chromosomal, neurological and congenital disorders among others. Typically, specialists such as pediatric endocrinologists inspect visually an X-ray of the non-dominant hand of the child and compare them against examples from atlases to estimate bone age and predict adult height.

Smart Pooling

Smart Pooling is an Artificial Intelligence (AI) system that increases the number of COVID-19 samples that can be tested with the same amount of resources. We use clinical and sociodemographic information of patients`to train an AI model to predict the probability of a sample being positive for COVID-19. With this information, we propose a novel pooled testing protocol that could improve efficiency for prevalences up to 50%. Smart Pooling is an innovative application of Machine Learning that augments the efficiency of testing regardless of the prevalence of COVID-19.

Lung Cancer Diagnosis

Lung cancer is the second most common type of cancer in the world. The reason for this staggering mortality rate is the near absolute absence of apparent symptoms in patients of early lung cancer. We develop novel methods for early detection of lung cancer that take into account multimodal information.

Heart Disease Diagnosis

Congenital heart defects (CHD) are changes in the function and shape of the heart that are developed during cardiac embryogenesis and are evident at birth. However, congenital heart defects are not detected efficiently, which is why they cause 24% of deaths in the first year of infants’ life. We propose an Artificial Intelligence algorithm that accompanies doctors in the diagnosis of congenital heart defects. This project is part of the national program DELFOS for detection of CHD in Colombia
